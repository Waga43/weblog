Learn Markdown

Markdown is the "language" in which those in the IT world write and communicate. It is a markup language and not a programming language per se. Learning markdown syntax is a very invaluable skill in this field. Even if you don’t have any plans on having any data skills, trust me when I tell you it is in your best interest to learn the markdown syntax. If you like the design of this website, then I am happy to let you know that this entire blogpost with its formatting was written in markdown. It can be learnt from between 2 days to about a week. Below are some resources to help you get started using Markdown. Refer to the guide by Markdown Guide website for general and detailed information about what you can use markdown for.

Structure of the DataCamp Certifications

DataCamp offers the following tracks for learners to choose from so they get the best custom-based learning experience:

  1. Organization Track: A collection of well-curated courses by yourscholarshipp organization or DataCamp, to guide your learning and help you gain new skills.

  2. Skill Track: Acquire new skills fast in courses that combine short expert videos with immediate hands-on exercises. This is based on particular skill sets learners wish to acquire.

  3. Career Track consists of hand-picked courses by industry experts. You will learn all you need to start a new career in the data science field. This is based on the career options of the learners.

DataCamp Certifications

Take the next step in your career and prove your competencies with DataCamp recognized career, technology, and fundamentals certifications (shown above). If you’re not quite ready for that next step, you can also prepare yourself with some tailored learning plans.

DataCamp Certifications

DataCamp certifications are developed in partnership with industry experts. They can help expand career opportunities, increase earning potential, and demonstrate expertise.

Duration

You’ll have 30 days or 2 attempts to pass and get certified.

DataCamp offers both Associate and Professional certifications, designed to reflect entry-level and more experienced skill levels, respectively. The main difference between the two is the difficulty of the questions and the final exam requirements.

  • Associate certification: Designed for those starting out, and aligns with entry-level job expectations. It consists of a series of DataCamp assessments.

  • Professional certification: Designed for those with at least two years of experience. It requires a written and verbal presentation for the final exam.

  • DataCamp Learning Plans towards Certifications: DataCamp allows you to build a plan from scratch or take assessments to target lessons specifically for you.

Register for a DataCamp Certification

Number of Attempts

Once you begin a certification you will have 30 days or 2 attempts to complete all requirements, whichever occurs first. If you’re unable to complete the requirements within 30 days from starting, your progress will reset.
